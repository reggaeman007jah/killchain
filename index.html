Operation Killchain

This is a live Arma 3 project - i.e. it is still being actively developed but it also currently in good playable shape. The overall aim here is to create an MP environment where ground forces can work alongside helicopter (transport and CAS) elements in order to complete patrol objectives.

Operation Killchain - Detailed Briefing

Mission Background
This mission is an ongoing project of mine, designed to enable an easy and free-flowing mission setting, that brings players together during these crazy times. It can be played solo, with a team, there is even scope to play PVP (althoug untested). The basic structure is an ongoing patrol, where players assist automated AI patrols push through enemy terratory, providing support in ways that suit the players. The overall aim here is community - bringing together players of different abilities and play-styles.

I really hope you enjoy the experience - please always feel free to contact me via discord to share comments, experiences, bug reports, or just say hello :)

Cheers, Reggs

Core Framework and Mission Mechanics

This mission is designed to operate without any player interaction. blue and Red teams are created automatically, and go head to head. At the time of writing there is no set end-state (this will probably change), but for now this is essentially a perpetual patrol / push into enemy terratory.

The blue team always starts at their main FOB - here you will find vehicles, refuel, repair, rearm, virtual arsenal - all the usual stuff you'd want from an FOB.

On mission start, Indifor forces will be spawned and will head out on patrol. At the same time, opfor will also spawn at a camp closeby. Indifor and Opfpr will go head to head - here indifor are attacking and iopfor are defending.

Indifor will push forward until their numbders are diminished - when the force size reaches critical levels, more RF are spawned at the nearest friednly base, and move out to try to take the opfor camp. This push continues until Indifor are successful.

On taking the camp, Indifor then dig in to prepare for Opfor retalliation. this can come from a number of sidderent directions, and the size of the attack varies also. If Indifor manage to repell Opfor QRF, they will move onto the next camp objective.

The more bases that are taken, the longer the chain (hence the name, which is also a cyber security term), and eventually the mission will need to reply on this chain being unbroken in order to leverage benefits (like respawn positions).


Reward Systems

When an enemy camp is taken, and successfully defended, the camp is removed and replaced with a healing tent, a VA arsenal box, a vehicle repair container, and also a vehicle or two (vehicle rewards rank up over time)

In addition, this camp becomes a new resapwn point, so players can select this new base as a starting point


Player UX

Player UX (User Experience) is super important to me. First off, any comments good or bad are always welcomed - I know this will never appeal to everyone, but there may be things I/we can do to address game imbalences, or missing elements.

The mission has lots of helis - this is just the way the mission has evolved, and in many ways is designed on some of the books I have read covering Scout pilots operating in the Vietnam War. There is certainly lots of CAS options for pilots, and with a quick respawn system in place you are never far away from the action. The current mission hosts mainly AH-6 models, but there are some MH-6 helis for transportation, as well as a Huron and Hellcat. There is lots of scope to increae mission challenges for pilots (logitics, slingloading, transport, medivac etc ... all on the to-do list), but for now the focus is mainly CAS.

The mission also works well with ground operatorrs. Here you can play as a squad, fighting alongside the automated Indifor units. You can go straight to the ftonr, or stay back as suppression or DMR/Sniper roles.

The area I am very imterested in exploring is combined arms - FAC style roles. This is (in basic terms) a collaboration between ground forces and CAS elements.

I acknowledge that this mission may not be well suited for driver experiences - this is something I am planning to address in future.
